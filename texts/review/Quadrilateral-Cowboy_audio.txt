<p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;">On one side Quadrilateral Cowboy's audio it excellent, particularly concerning the mechanical parts. The tapping keys as you insert your code, the mechanical whine of the Weevils servos, the blaring alarms, they all sound great.</p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;">The various pieces of background audio sound good as well, even if they are limited. The breathing of the character while in a toxic atmosphere, the plink of screws as the fall from an overhead vent and the chirps of birds in a number of notable areas.</p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;">Unfort<span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium;">unately the game misses a great opportunity with it's music. The player has access to a walkman for records which is a great idea but for a game set within an alternate 80's the music choices are somewhat disappointing. I was hoping for some retro styled synthwave or classic pop songs but instead I got a small number a dreary depressing songs that felt very much out of place with the world of high tech hacking and tiny robots.</span></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;"><br></p><p style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); font-family: Tinos; font-size: medium; text-align: start;">Apart from the music Quadrilateral Cowboy is a good sounding game, the audio effects helping to establish the world as a real place, if only the music could have lived up to its setting.</p>
