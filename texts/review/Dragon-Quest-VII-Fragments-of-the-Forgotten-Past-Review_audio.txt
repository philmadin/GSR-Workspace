<br><i>Dragon Quest</i> has some of the best music to ever grace a videogame, and you can thank the supremely talented Koichi Sugiyama for that (who, incidentally, just achieved a world record for oldest videogame composer at 85!).<br><br><br>Though the original Japanese orchestral arrangement has been replaced with a MIDI track, such disappointment is alleviated somewhat by the high quality sound, and the sheer catchiness of the game's countless tunes. Unlike its sister franchise <i>Final Fantasy</i>, <i>Dragon Quest's</i> sound is typified by its brassy, big band style combined with 16-bit beeps that relish in its refusal to sound 'modern'.<br>
