The premise is simple yet sensationally executed. You play as an unnamed child who is distinguished primarily by his red shirt in a world of black and white. This <span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">child traverses a series of fantastical 2.5D environments while escaping danger, overcoming obstacles and trying to figure out just what the hell is going on. </span><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">I'm intentionally trying to be as vague as possible, the less you know going in to </span><i>Inside</i><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> the better. What I will say is that <i style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Playdead</i> is not afraid to really get itself out there in terms of storytelling. Strange things will happen and they will keep you on the edge of your seat, completely engrossed. The game also rewards adventurous players with a 'secret ending' that I would highly recommend seeking out.</span>
