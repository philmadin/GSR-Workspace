Eschewing an English dub in favour of keeping the Japanese voices, I feel, was the right choice by Nintendo and Atlus. Not only does it keep the Japanese setting's authenticity, but it also avoided a year-long delay for (let's face it) a verbal translation that really wasn't necessary with something as niche as this.<br><br>The quality of the music depends on whether you like J-pop, and funky, bass-heavy beats. Personally, I do not, but I nevertheless found the whole thing a pleasure to listen to. Everything is so upbeat, it masked the sometimes-repetitive gameplay.<br>
