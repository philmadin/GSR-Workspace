<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height:150%"><br></p><p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height:150%"><span style="font-family:" arial",sans-serif"="">This week has been filled with excitement for all first-person shooter fans with announcement dates and rumours for two major franchises spilling onto the internet.<o:p></o:p></span></p><p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height:150%"><span style="font-family:" arial",sans-serif"="">On April 30<sup>th</sup> <font face="Arial, sans-serif"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gaDJX0Ttsfc">a teaser was released on the official Call of Duty YouTube</a></font> channel containing in-game cinematic footage of a man delivering a foreboding message to the audience. The teaser has received mixed messages from the community with a proportionate number of dislikes on the video. This is more than likely due to the futuristic setting of the annual <i style="font-family:">Call of Duty</i> as evident from the trailer, of which players seem to becoming sick of. This teaser builds on rumours of a <i style="font-family:">Call of Duty: Modern Warfare remastered edition&nbsp;</i>which is expected to be launched alongside their major release this year. <o:p></o:p></span></p><p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height:150%"><span style="font-family:" arial",sans-serif"="">For veteran <i style="font-family:">Call of Duty</i> players this prospect has had them licking their lips in the lead up to the impending annual announcement. In many ways this will serve as proxy market research for Activision, as depending on the success of the remastered edition, will determine which direction future <i style="font-family:">Call of Duty</i> instalments will take.
