<p><br></p>Exciting news today as Nintendo reveals more Pokemon and features, plus new forms for existing Pokemon in <i><b>Sun</b> <b>and</b></i> <b><i>Moon</i></b>.&nbsp;<p><br></p><p><br></p><p><br><br>While in the Alola region, some existing Pokemon have an Alola Form. This form changes both the look and typing of that Pokemon. The trailer shows Exeggutor become a Grass/Dragon type, Vulpix (Ice), Ninetails (Ice/Fairy), Sandshrew (Ice/Steel) and Sandslash (Ice/Steel).&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><br><br>Alongside this, more Pokemon from the Alola region were revealed. Oricorio, a cute flying type with a different form based on the region it is found in. Minior is a Rock/Flying type that sheds its outer shell to reveal a colourful core. There is also Gumshoos (Normal), Formantis (Grass), Lorantis (Grass) and Mudbray (Ground).&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><br><br>One of the new features shown was Poke Ride, the ability to call a Pokemon to help reach certain places. These Pokemon don't take a spot on your team but can be used any time. Pokemon now also have a Z-Move, a once per battle attack that deals massive damage and is unique to each type.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><br><br>While traveling around the Alola region, you must pass a trial on each island in order to complete the Island Challenge. These trials vary from item hunts to puzzles. Once completed, you face a Totem Pokemon with a special aura, then the final trial, a battle against the island leader.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><br><br><i><b>Pokemon Sun and Moon </b></i>will be released November 18.<br><br></p>
