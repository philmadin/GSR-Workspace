Sony has <a href="http://www.jp.playstation.com/info/release/nr-20171002-psvr.html">announced</a> that the Japanese release date for their new PlayStation VR headset (model number CUH-ZVR2) will be October 14th, 2017. It will be sold alongside in a bundle with the PlayStation Camera for 44,980 yen.<p><br></p><p><br></p><p>The new model is designed to enable stereo headphones to be integrated with the headset and an updated Processor Unit to support HDR pass through. This means users can stream their HDR-compatible PlayStation 4 content to a TV without needing to disconnect the Processor Unit in between the TV and console.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>VR enthusiasts who already own the original PlayStation VR headset (model number CUH-ZVR1) will not be able to swap out their old Processor Units with the updated one due to the incompatible cables used. Those who opt to buy the new model, however, will be able to enjoy their VR games (as well as upcoming VR compatible titles) with both the original and updated headsets.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>Sony has confirmed there will be a North American product launch at a <a href="https://blog.us.playstation.com/2017/10/02/playstation-vr-the-ultimate-faq/">"later date"</a>.</p>
