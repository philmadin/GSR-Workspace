<p>If you're heading to Japan to check out the <a href="http://gamesharkreviews.com/news.php?t=Final_Fantasy_VR_roller_coaster%21"><i>Final Fantasy</i> VR roller coaster</a> next year, be sure to check out the official <a href="http://www.finalfantasy.jp/30th/exhibition/">'<i>Final Fantasy</i> 30th Anniversary Exhibition'</a> in Tokyo's Roppongi Hills. Developer Square Enix has announced that although 2017 marks the 30th anniversary of the franchise, the art exhibit will be scheduled to run from January 22nd to February 28th, 2018.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>'Stories of Farewell' will be the theme for the art exhibit. Fans can expect to find official art examining the beautiful tear-jerking farewell moments throughout the game's thirty-year run. <i>Final Fantasy</i> <i>XV </i>producer, Shinji Hashimoto, says:</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">"Why is it that, in every <i style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Final Fantasy</i> game, there are scenes that show separation? Because as we live life, bonds and friendships are born, but there will inevitably come a time when we have to say good-bye. Tales of separation, they are also tales of meeting and connecting with new people, tales of conflict, trials, and battle. They are an expression of life itself."</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"><br></span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"><br></span></p><p><a href="https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000004.000025962.html">Details</a> for ticket sales have not been announced yet but the 'Mori Arts Center Gallery' (Roppongi Hills Mori Tower 52F) will be hosting the event.</p>
