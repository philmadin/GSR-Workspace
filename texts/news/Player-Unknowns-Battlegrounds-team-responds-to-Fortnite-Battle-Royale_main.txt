<span style="font-size: 16px">Bluehole, Inc., developers of <b style="font-size: 16px"><i style="font-size: 16px">PLAYERUNKNOWN'S BATTLEGROUNDS (PUBG)</i></b>, the first standalone Battle Royale survival shooter game, responded today to growing concerns regarding the similarities between the battle royale mode in Epic Games' newly revealed <i style="font-size: 16px">Fortnite</i> and <b style="font-size: 16px"><i style="font-size: 16px">PUBG</i></b>.<br><br style="font-size: 16px">"We've had an ongoing relationship with Epic Games throughout PUBG's development as they are the creators of UE4, the engine we licensed for the game," said Chang Han Kim, Vice President and Executive Producer for Bluehole, Inc. "After listening to the growing feedback from our community and reviewing the gameplay for ourselves, we are concerned that Fortnite may be replicating the experience for which PUBG is known."<br></span><p><span style="font-size: 16px;"><font color="#505050"><br style="font-size: 16px"></font>The development team for <b style="font-size: 16px"><i style="font-size: 16px">PUBG</i></b> has no relationship to <i style="font-size: 16px">Fortnite'</i>s development as concerns around User Interface (UI), gameplay and structural replication in the battle royale mode exist.<br style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;">"We have also noticed that Epic Games references PUBG in the promotion of Fortnite to their community and in communications with the press," Chang Han Kim continues. "This was never discussed with us and we don't feel that it's right."<br></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;"><font color="#505050"><br style="font-size: 16px"></font>With its release on March 23, 2017, <b style="font-size: 16px"><i style="font-size: 16px">PLAYERUNKNOWN'S BATTLEGROUNDS</i></b> is a last man standing shooter that drops players on a realistic 8 x 8 km island where they start with nothing in an ever-diminishing field of play. Gamers must fight to locate weapons, vehicles and supplies as they strive to be the lone survivor.<br style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;">In a recent Reddit AMA (e.g. Ask Me Anything) session, Brendan Greene (aka PLAYERUKNOWN) was asked about other companies replicating the Battle Royale experience:<br style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;"><i style="font-size: 16px">"Other companies will, of course, enter the marketplace, but I would just hope they put their own spin on the game mode and not just make a carbon copy!"</i><br style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;">Chang Han Kim closed, "The PUBG community has and continues to provide evidence of the many similarities as we contemplate further action."<br style="font-size: 16px"></span></p>
