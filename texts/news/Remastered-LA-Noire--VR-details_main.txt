Rockstar surprised their twitter followers with some exciting <a href="https://www.rockstargames.com/newswire/article/60266/4-New-Versions-of-L-A-NOIRE-Coming-November-14?utm_source=twitter&amp;utm_medium=social&amp;utm_campaign=lanoire-09072017&amp;utm_term=newswire">news</a> recently. Their 2011 Australian made blockbuster game <i>L.A. Noire </i>is set to make a comeback in November. The game is being remastered for the Xbox One, PlayStation 4 and Nintendo Switch, as well as getting its own VR release.<p><br><p><br></p><p>For the Xbox One and the PlayStation 4 versions, <i>L.A. Noire</i> will include DLCs plus the main game that has been enhanced to support 1080p graphics (boosted to 4k if you're playing on a PS4 Pro/Xbox One X). Noticeable improvements include lightning and cloud visuals, realistic textiles, new cinematic angles and more.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>For the Nintendo Switch, Rockstar has tried to make full use of the console's different play style. They have added a Joy-Con mode, gesture-based controls, new wide over-the-shoulder camera angles and contextual touch screen controls.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>A VR title, <i>L.A. Noire: The VR Case Files</i>, will be Rockstar's first step into VR development. It will comprise of seven select cases from the original game ported into virtual reality for the HTC VIVE.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"><br></span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Set during the 1940s, </span><i>L.A. Noire</i><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> is an adventure detective game following the story of a fresh faced detective Cole Phelps as he investigates a series of violent crimes.&nbsp;</span><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">The game will be available on November 14th 2017.</span></p></p>
