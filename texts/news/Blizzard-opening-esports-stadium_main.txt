<p>October 7th will see Blizzard Entertainment's first United States esports stadium open in Los Angeles. The world renowned games' developer is known for making popular esports titles such as <i>World of Warcraft</i>, <i>Overwatch</i> and <i>Heroes of the Storm</i>.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>Earlier this year in March, Blizzard opened its first stadium (dubbed the 'Blizzard Estadium') in Taipei, Taiwan. It can seat between 200 and 500 spectators and houses a 6.144 x 3.456-metre main display screen. Its' first official event was the Overwatch Pacific Championship.</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>Next month's new arena 'Blizzard Arena Los Angeles' will be part of The Burbank Studios, a television production facility. Blizzard news reported: "Multiple broadcasts and events can be run out of Blizzard Arena simultaneously, thanks to the multiple sound stages, control rooms and player lounges throughout the facility. The on-site Blizzard store will open during events and feature a rotating selection of gear and goods based on the event taking place at the time."</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>It will host the <i>Overwatch</i> Contenders Playoffs competition as the grand opening event (7th &amp; 8th October). Scheduled closely behind are the <i>Hearthstone</i> Championship Tour's Summer Championship, the <i>Heroes of the Storm</i> Global Championship Finals and the <i>World of Warcraft</i> Arena Championship.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><a href="https://worldcup.playoverwatch.com/en-us/#group-stage">Tickets</a> are already available for both the <i>Overwatch</i> and <i>Hearthstone</i> events.</p>
