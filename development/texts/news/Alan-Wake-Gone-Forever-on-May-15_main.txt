<p><i>Alan Wake</i>, the acclaimed psychological thriller from Remedy Entertainment, may soon be disappearing forever.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p><i>Alan Wake</i> was received positively by critics and audiences alike upon its release, receiving Time Magazine's 2010 Game of the Year Award and the IGN's Editors' Choice Award. Unfortunately, following expired licensing agreements on music that appears in the game, the praised title is due to be permanently removed from all digital storefronts.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>On a Q&amp;A discussion on the Remedy Forums, a community spokesperson stated that while they are "looking into relicensing the music for Alan Wake," they currently "have no timeframe for this."&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>In a final attempt to sell copies, <i>Alan Wake</i> and the follow-up title <i>Alan Wake's American Nightmare</i> have received a 90% discount on digital distribution platforms Steam, GOG, and the Xbox 360 Marketplace. The sale began on the May 13, and will conclude on May 15 17:00 UTC, after which the game will be gone forever. However, while the discount will end on <i>American Nightmare</i>, that title will not be removed.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>If you already own <i>Alan Wake</i>, there's no cause for alarm - the game will not be removed from your library and will continue to run as normal.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p><br></p><p>If you've never played the series before and think you might want to - make sure to grab your digital copy now before it's too late.</p>
