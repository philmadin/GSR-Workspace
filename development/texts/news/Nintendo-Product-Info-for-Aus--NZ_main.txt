<p></p><p>April's <a href="https://www.nintendo.com.au/arms-and-splatoon-2-headline-new-nintendo-direct-presentation" target="_blank" title="This external link will open in a new window">Nintendo Direct</a> presentation revealed exciting details about several new and upcoming <a href="https://www.nintendo.com.au/nintendo-switch/" target="_blank" title="This external link will open in a new window">Nintendo Switch</a> and <a href="https://www.nintendo.com.au/nintendo-3ds" target="_blank" title="This external link will open in a new window">Nintendo 3DS</a> games, and nine new additions to the <a href="http://amiibo.nintendo.com.au/index.php?c=home" target="_blank" title="This external link will open in a new window">amiibo</a> range. Today, Nintendo announces the latest release dates for three anticipated releases in Australia and New Zealand:</p><p></p><br><br><p></p><p>- Nintendo Switch Dock Set: Including a Nintendo Switch dock, HDMI cable and AC adaptor, the Nintendo Switch Dock Set allows for the Nintendo Switch to be played in TV mode, and also charges the Nintendo Switch and Joy-Con. The Nintendo Switch Dock Set is identical to the Nintendo Switch dock, HDMI cable and AC adaptor that is included with the Nintendo Switch system, and is ideal for those with multiple TVs. The Nintendo Switch Dock Set will launch on 26th May for a suggested retail price of AU$129.95.</p><br><br>- Super Smash Bros. amiibo: Initially announced as releasing on 22nd July, the final Super Smash Bros. amiibo figures will now launch a day earlier on 21st of July. Two versions of Cloud, Corrin and Bayonetta amiibo figures will hit store shelves alongside alongside the three new Splatoon amiibo figures - Inkling Girl, Inkling Boy and Inkling Squid - for SRP AU$17.95 each.<br><br>- Dr Kawashima's Devilish Brain Training: Can you stay focused?: The third entry in the series, this game seeks to train players' brains with a series of challenging concentration-based exercises. The game will be released on 29th July for SRP AU$49.95.<p></p>
